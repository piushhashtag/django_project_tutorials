Description:-

Op-amp and 555 ICs are widely used for generating PWM signals.
This circuit as a dimmer switch for LED lights. This PWM concept is also applicable for lamp dimmer switch too.
At the instant of powering ON the circuit, output will be 5V, because the voltage at the 2nd pin (trigger pin) is less than 1/3 Vcc.
At the next moment, the output voltage will reach the capacitor via the 10k of potentiometer and diode D2 so that the capacitor starts charging with a time constant Rd R1C(where Rd is the forward resistance of the diode D2).
When the capacitor voltage exceeds 2/3Vcc, the 555 timer gets reset. Then the output will be 0V.
At the moment the capacitor discharges via the diode D1 and potentiometer R1 to the output pin since it is in ground potential.
When the capacitor voltage goes below 1/3 Vcc, the output of 555 IC again rises to 5V this process continues.
Here the charging and discharging path is entirely different since it is isolated by diodes D1 and D2.if the potentiometer midpoint is at 50% we will able to get 50% duty cycle.
Pulse with can be changing the charging and discharging time, this is possible by adjusting the potentiometer. Thus we get PWM signal.
